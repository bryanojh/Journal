    Nasi Lemak is a dish that originated from our neighbours, Malaysia! It is a very popular dish here in Singapore as well due to it's many flavours as well as being able to be eaten at any time of the day be it for breakfast, lunch, dinner or supper! It is a dish that can be found in any Hawker Centres or Shopping Centres.

    All this talk about Nasi Lemak must be making you hungry! Scroll below to find out what ingredients are needed as well as how to prepare and create your very own Nasi Lemak at home, have fun cooking and enjoy the delicious result!

    Ingredients needed:

    Ayam Goreng Berempah (Spiced Fried Chicken)

    • 2 lemongrass, cut
    • 4 garlic cloves
    • ½ inch ginger
    • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
    • 1 teaspoon ground fennel
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 4 chicken legs/ thighs, or 2-3 breasts
    • ¼ cup coconut milk (60 ml)
    • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
    • 2 tablespoon rice flour
    Sambal Tumis Ikan Bilis (Anchovies Sambal)
    • 3.5 oz dry anchovies (100 gr)
    • 2 red onions , sliced
    • 1.8 oz dry chilli, cut into small pieces and remove the seeds. (50 gr), place chilli in a bowl add hot water and leave to soak for 1 hour. Rinse and drain the chilli, set aside.
    • 3 shallots, roughly cut
    • 4 garlic cloves
    • ½ inch ginger
    • ½ inch shrimp paste
    • water
    • sunflower oil
    • 1 teaspoon salt, hold back the salt as anchovies are already salty
    • 3 tablespoon sugar
    • 2 tablespoon tamarind juice or use lemon/lime juice to taste
    Nasi Lemak (Rice with coconut milk)
    • 2 cups basmati rice (370 gr)
    • 3 cups canned coconut milk (750 ml)
    • 1 cup water (250 ml)
    • 1 pandan leaf, knotted
    • 1 shallot, thinly sliced
    • 2 inch ginger, cut into matchsticks
    • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
    • 1 lemongrass, cut into 2 inch length
    • 2 teaspoon salt
    • water for soaking rice
      • Sides (optional)
    • hard boiled eggs
    • sliced cucumber
    • peanuts

    Follow these easy steps in order to make your very own Nasi Lemak from the comfort of your home!

    Ayam Goreng Berempah (Spiced Fried Chicken)
    1. Blend/pound lemongrass, garlic and ginger into a paste. Set aside.
    2. In a bowl add in chicken, pounded paste and the rest of the ingredients except egg and rice flour. Mix everything well. Marinate overnight or at least 4 hours.
    3. Add egg and rice flour to marinated chicken, coating them well. Set aside.
    4. Heat a pot of oil on medium heat. Fry chicken until golden brown and fully cooked. Place paper towel to drain the excess oil.
    Sambal Tumis Ikan Bilis (Anchovies Sambal)
    1. Blend chilli, shallots, garlic, shrimp paste and ginger with a splash of water ,into a paste. Set aside.
    2. Heat some oil in a pot and fry the anchovies until they are crispy. Place on paper towel, set aside. Then fry the onions until they are soft and golden brown at the edges. Place on a plate, set aside.
    3. Pour in the blended chilli and add in a splash of water to loosen it up a bit. Cook for 15-20 minutes until the sauce reduces and thickens (stir now and then).
    4. Season with salt, sugar and tamarind juice (a good sambal needs to be a bit sweet with a balance of sour and salty). Add in fried anchovies and onion. Turn the heat off and mix well. Sambal is ready.
    Nasi Lemak (Rice with Coconut Milk)
    1. Soak basmati rice with water for 30 minutes. Drain right before using.
    2. Place the rest of the ingredients in a pot and bring to a boil, then add in rice. Bring it back to a simmer, cover pot with a lid and cook on low heat for 20 minutes. Remove from the heat and leave it for another 10 minutes before fluffing up the rice and serve.
    Things to note:
    1. Change the ratio (coconut milk : water) if you prefer the rice to be less buttery.
      1. For basmati rice 2 cup coconut milk:2 cup water or 1 cup coconut milk:3 cup water.
      2. For jasmine rice use 2 cup coconut milk:1 cup water or 1 cup coconut milk:2 cup water.
    2. Use any other protein source that you like for sambal (no need to fry first except chicken).
